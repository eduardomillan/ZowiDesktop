Zowi Desktop — User Guide

Zowi Desktop is a cross-platform application to control and program your Zowi robot from a computer over Bluetooth. This guide walks you through every screen of the app.

1. Choose your language

When the app opens you see the splash screen. Pick your language from the list — Español, Valencià, English, Français, Български — and press Continue. You can also Quit the app from here.

Splash screen with language selector

2. Welcome

The welcome screen introduces Zowi. Press Start to begin the setup, or Learn more to read about the robot.

Welcome screen

3. Turn on your Zowi

The setup wizard checks that your Zowi is ready. Make sure it is turned on and nearby. If it is already on, choose “It's already on”. If you don't own a Zowi yet, you can still explore the app without one.

Setup wizard — turn on Zowi

4. Connect over Bluetooth

The app scans for nearby Zowi devices. When your Zowi appears in the list, tap it. You will be asked to enter the pairing code 1234 to pair with the robot.

Scanning for Zowis
Pair with your Zowi (code 1234)

5. Name your Zowi

Type a friendly name for your Zowi and press Rename. The name is sent to the robot and remembered for next time.

Naming your Zowi

6. Home screen

Once connected you reach the home screen, which has two pages:

  • Zowi Apps — games and interactive modes.
  • Projects — programming activities.

Use the top buttons to open Settings and Achievements. The status bar at the top shows the current connection state:

  • Connected · <name> · <battery>% · <MAC> — your Zowi is ready.
  • Connecting… — the app is trying to reach your Zowi.
  • Zowi unavailable — your saved Zowi is off or out of range; tap Reconnect.
  • Not connected — no Zowi has been set up.
Home — Zowi Apps
Home — Projects

7. Automatic reconnection

Every time you open the app, Zowi Desktop tries to reconnect to the last used Zowi if it is powered on. If it is off, the status bar will tell you, and you can press Reconnect at any time.

8. Reset (developer mode)

The Reset button, shown in developer mode, removes the registered Zowi from the app and from the Bluetooth system. If no Zowi is registered, a warning message is shown instead.
